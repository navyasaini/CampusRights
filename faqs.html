Frequently Asked Questions

  • Campus rights refer to the legal and ethical protections afforded to students within their educational institution. These rights include academic freedom, freedom of expression, privacy rights, and protections against discrimination.

  • You can assert your rights by familiarizing yourself with your institution's policies and procedures. If you believe your rights are being violated, you can seek guidance from student affairs offices or legal services on campus.

  • Report any instances of discrimination to the appropriate campus office, such as the Office of Equity and Inclusion or the Title IX coordinator. They can provide support and guidance on addressing your concerns.

  • Financial aid includes scholarships, grants, loans, and work-study programs. These resources help students cover tuition, fees, and living expenses while pursuing higher education.

  • You can apply for financial aid by completing the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) and any additional forms required by your college or university. Be sure to meet application deadlines for each academic year.

  • Scholarships are typically merit-based or awarded based on specific criteria such as academic achievement or extracurricular involvement. Grants, on the other hand, are usually need-based and do not require repayment.

  • Privacy rights include protections over your personal information, educational records (under FERPA), and digital communications. You have the right to know how your data is collected, used, and shared by your educational institution.

  • Protect your digital privacy by using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, and being cautious about sharing personal information online. Familiarize yourself with your college's policies on data security and online privacy.

  • Report any suspected privacy violations to the appropriate campus authority, such as the privacy officer or student affairs office. They can investigate the issue and take necessary actions to address the violation.

  • Sexual harassment includes unwanted sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature that creates a hostile or intimidating environment.

  • You can report sexual harassment to the Title IX coordinator or the Office of Student Conduct. They will guide you through the reporting process and provide support services available to you.

  • Victims of sexual harassment can access counseling services, legal assistance, and accommodations through their college's Title IX office or health services. Confidential support resources are also available.

  • Academic integrity refers to the ethical principles and values that govern academic work, including honesty, fairness, and respect for intellectual property. It encompasses avoiding plagiarism, cheating, and unauthorized collaboration.

  • Academic integrity ensures fairness in evaluation and assessment, maintains the credibility of academic achievements, and upholds the reputation of educational institutions. It fosters a culture of honesty and ethical conduct among students.

  • Examples of academic misconduct include plagiarism (using someone else's work without proper citation), cheating on exams or assignments, submitting falsified data or information, and unauthorized collaboration on assignments.

  • To avoid plagiarism, always cite sources properly when using someone else's ideas, words, or research findings. Use quotation marks for direct quotes and include citations for paraphrased information. Familiarize yourself with citation styles required by your institution.

  • If you suspect academic misconduct, such as plagiarism or cheating, report your concerns to the professor or teaching assistant responsible for the course. They will investigate the issue according to the institution's academic integrity policies.

  • Consequences of academic misconduct may include failing grades on assignments or exams, academic probation, suspension, or expulsion from the institution. It can also impact your future academic and professional opportunities.

  • Academic integrity in online learning requires students to uphold the same standards of honesty and ethical behavior as in traditional classroom settings. It involves avoiding unauthorized collaboration, using credible sources, and respecting academic guidelines for online assessments.

  • You can find resources on academic integrity, including policies, guidelines, and tutorials, on your institution's website or through the academic affairs or student services offices. These resources provide guidance
