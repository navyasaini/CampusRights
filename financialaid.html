Financial Aid for College Students

College students can get financial support for institution fees, hostel fees, and other expenses through various scholarships. One of the primary objectives of the Government is to ensure that no student is deprived of the opportunity to pursue higher education due to financial constraints. To achieve this, the Department of Higher Education (DHE) has launched various schemes that provide financial assistance to economically disadvantaged students. These schemes include scholarships, interest subsidies on education loans, and credit guarantees for education loans. Students from all categories (General, SC, ST, OBC, and PwD) are eligible for financial assistance in accordance with the specific scheme norms.

The National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 also aims to increase the Gross Enrolment Ratio in higher education from approximately 27% in 2019-20 to 50% by 2035. This goal underscores the importance of encouraging students, particularly those from Economically Weaker Sections of society, to continue their education. This requires sustained policy initiatives.

The financial assistance schemes of the DHE are part of the umbrella scheme “Pradhan Mantri Uchchatar Shiksha Protsahan (PM-USP) Yojana”. For more information on scholarships and fellowships offered by the UGC and AICTE, please visit their respective websites.
