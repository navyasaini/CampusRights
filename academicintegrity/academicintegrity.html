Academic Integrity

Academic integrity is crucial for maintaining a fair and honest educational environment. It ensures that students engage in ethical behavior by avoiding cheating, plagiarism, and other dishonest practices. Understanding and upholding academic integrity is essential, as violations can lead to serious consequences such as failing grades or expulsion. This section provides resources and guidelines to help you navigate academic expectations and uphold your rights responsibly.

Regulation for Unfair Means

Unfair means use of any dishonest means or indulgence in disorderly conduct in the examination. It includes

  1. Assisting in any manner whatsoever any other candidate in answering the question paper during the course of the examination
  2. Taking assistance from any other candidate or any other person or from any back paper notes or other material in answering the question paper during the course of the examination
  3. Carrying any book, paper, notes, or other material whatsoever likely to be used directly or indirectly by the candidate in connection with the examination, in the examination centre
  4. Smuggling in an answer book or a continuation sheet.
  5. Taking out or arranging to send out an answer book or its any page or continuation sheet.
  6. Replacing or getting replaced an answer book or its any page or continuation sheet during or after the examination.
  7. >Getting impersonated by any person in the examination.
  8. Deliberately disclosing one's identify or making any distinctive mark in the answer book for that purpose.
  9. Communicating with or talking to any other candidate or unauthorized person in or around the examination centre during the course of the examination.
  10. Communicating or attempting to communicate directly or through a relative, guardian and friend with an examiner with the object of influencing him in the award of marks.
  11. Swallowing or destroying any note or paper found with her.
  12. Note written on any part of clothing, body, desk, table, or any instrument such as setsquare, Instrument box, calculator, loose paper, admit card, question paper, photocopies of printed / written matter and use of electronic gadget like mobile phones etc.
  13. Consulting notes, books or any other outside person while going to toilet, etc. outside the Examination centre.

If any student is caught for use of unfair means shall have to give a statement to the centre superintendent in presence of the respecitve inviligator. The superintendent shall have the power to expel the candidate from examination centre for remaining duration of paper.

A person appearing in place of an actual candidate shall invite registration of criminal case under Indian penal code. Such candiate may be debarred from appearing at any future exam of the university or can be expelled from the university and declared not fit or peroper person to be admitted to any other university

Attendance

A student has to put in minimum of 75% attendance in aggregate of all the courses taken together in a semester Dean of faculty may condone attendance shortage upto 10% for individual student for reasons to be recorded in writing. The Vice chancellor may further relax the minimum attendance upto five percent under exceptional circumstances.

The relaxation provided is under given circumstances:

  1. Busy in authorised activites
  2. Suffering from any disease/disorder. A student should submit the documents to above effect with seven days of resuming the studies

Student who has been detained shall not be allowed to be promoted to next semester and will have to repeat all courses of the said semester with next batch of students. In case any student appears by default who in fact has been detained by the department, her result shall be treated as null and void.

Criteria for passing courses

  • Obtaining a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate in each course including the end term exam and teacher's continuous evaluation shall be essential for passing the course. Otherwise the student shall be deemed to have failed in that course.
  • A student may apply within two weeks from the date of declaration of the result for rechecking of the examination. Rechecking means verifying whether all questions and their parts have been duly marked and check totaling of the marks. If discrepnacy found, it shall be rectified
